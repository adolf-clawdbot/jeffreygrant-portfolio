Amazon Fulfillment Portal

UI/UX Design

Summary

Amazon's fulfillment operations relied on three disconnected systems: warehouse associate tools, Seller Central dashboards, and operations manager analytics. I led the design of Project Meridian—a unified portal that brought these experiences together into a single, cohesive platform built on Amazon's Cloudscape Design System.

My Role

This project took place between August 2023 and March 2024 (7 months). I worked as the Lead UX Designer on the Fulfillment Experience Team.

I was responsible for leading a team of 2 UX designers and 1 researcher, working alongside product managers, engineers, and business stakeholders. My responsibilities included user research, information architecture, interaction design, visual design, and design system development.

The Challenge

Fragmented Systems

Amazon's third-party sellers and warehouse associates were managing fulfillment operations across six disparate systems. This fragmentation created significant inefficiencies:

  • FC associates spending 18% of their shift navigating clunky interfaces
  • Sellers unable to track real-time inventory across warehouses
  • Operations managers manually reconciling data from multiple sources
  • New sellers requiring 6-8 weeks to become proficient with tools

Research

Understanding Three User Groups

Through 38 interviews and shadow sessions at three fulfillment centers (BFI4, SDF8, JFK8), we identified distinct pain points across user groups:

FC Associates: Screen timeouts while reading instructions, confusing navigation, lack of mobile accessibility for warehouse floor work.

FBA Sellers: Inventory data showing 6+ hour delays, buried alerts for stockouts, fragmented navigation across Seller Central.

Operations Managers: Spending 12+ hours/week stitching together reports from four different dashboards.

Design Approach

Key Design Decisions

1. Role-Based Dashboards — Different interfaces for associates (mobile-first, high contrast), sellers (desktop analytics), and managers (reporting tools).

2. Real-Time Data Sync — Inventory updates every 15 minutes instead of 6+ hours, reducing seller anxiety and stockout incidents.

3. Cloudscape Design System Extension — Built 14 custom React components on top of Amazon's open-source Cloudscape library for consistency.

Design System

Custom Components Created
  • InventoryHealthRing — Visual stock level indicator with color-coded zones
  • ShipmentTimeline — Step-by-step progress tracker for inbound shipments
  • PickTaskCard — Mobile-optimized associate task display
  • AlertPriorityBadge — P1-P4 severity indicators
  • FCLocationPicker — Multi-select warehouse filter

Mobile Experience

Warehouse-First Design

We designed a Progressive Web App with offline capability for FC associates. The interface features high-contrast colors, large touch targets (minimum 44px), and a warehouse mode optimized for bright industrial lighting conditions.

Results

Impact & Outcomes

Six months post-launch, Project Meridian delivered measurable improvements:

  • 68% faster associate task completion (12.7 sec → 4.1 sec avg)
  • 47% reduction in seller support tickets
  • $4.2M annual cost savings
  • 89/100 System Usability Scale (up from 61/100)
  • +38 Net Promoter Score (up from -12)

Reflection

Key Learnings

Early FC Shadowing: Spending 3 full shifts at fulfillment centers revealed critical usability issues with handheld scanners that we wouldn't have caught in lab testing.

Iterative Prototyping: We tested 4 dashboard layouts with 80+ users. The winner wasn't the team's favorite—but it was the users' clear choice.

What I'd Do Differently: We should have involved FC ops leaders earlier. We redesigned the manager reporting module twice because we didn't understand their compliance requirements upfront.
